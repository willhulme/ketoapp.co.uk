What is DKA?

Having high blood sugar might lead to something called Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA). DKA can be avoided with regular self-monitoring of blood glucose levels and ketone levels and making appropriate insulin therapy adjustments. Episodes of high blood glucose and ketones are usually infrequent, so an incident might make a person feel unsure on how to manage it and how to make the necessary insulin therapy adjustments.

How Does The KetoApp Help?

The KetoApp aims to provide a mobile phone application that will provide an easy way to help someone experiencing DKA. It guides a user through the steps of what to do next in case of high blood sugar or DKA by showing information, calculating the necessary insulin dosage and alerting the user at the appropriate time when the next insulin check should be performed.

The application is based on the current Ketocard, a fold-out card currently in use by NHS Highland, which provides the steps to be taken in the event of DKA. In addition, the Ketoapp also provides information related to hypoglycaemia.

Using State of the Art Technology

The KetoApp has been developed using Microsoft's cross-platform mobile app development & app creation software Xamarin. With a shared C# codebase, the KetoApp compiles natively to Android and iOS. The KetoApp was also designed from scratch with the Model-view-viewmodel software architectural pattern in mind, to facilitate a separation of the graphical user interface, and it's back-end logic.

Providing a Personalized Experience

Our phones know more about us than we do. That benefit can really shine through with the KetoApp. No longer is it the impersonal card sat inside your wallet, the KetoApp improves the care your diabetes team can give you by providing multiple medical-based profiles for you and your family within the app, giving personalized contact information of your diabetes ward, diabetologist, and out-of-hours phone numbers. The KetoApp also uses the information you provide to enhance its algorithm, for example, customizing its answers based upon an insulin pump or pen.
